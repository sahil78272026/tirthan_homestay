Places to Visit Near the Homestay

UNESCO World Heritage
🏔️
~15 km · Accessible from Banjar & Gushaini

Great Himalayan National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage Site spanning 754 sq km, home to Snow Leopards, Western Tragopan, Himalayan Bear, Blue Sheep and 375+ species. Trek into pristine alpine forests from Shairopa or Gushaini gates. One of India's finest protected wilderness areas.

TrekkingWildlifeUNESCOPhotography
🏰
~7 km from Banjar

Chehni Kothi Fort Tower

A 1,500-year-old fort tower built by King Rana Dhadhia — the tallest traditional Kath-Kuni structure in Himachal Pradesh. Originally 7 storeys, partially damaged in the 1905 earthquake. Trek ~1 hr from Shringa Rishi Temple through apple orchards and pine forests.

HistoryArchitectureTrek
🙏
~3 km from Banjar · on Jibhi road

Shringa Rishi Temple

The ruling deity of Banjar Valley. A stunning three-storey wooden pagoda-style temple dedicated to Sage Rishyashringa. Starting point for the Chehni Kothi trail. Walk 15 minutes from the road. Serene and deeply important to locals.

TempleCultureHeritage
🌊
~8 km · 30-min trek from Gushaini

Chhoie Waterfall

The highest waterfall in Tirthan Valley — a spectacular 50-metre cascade set deep in deodar and oak forest. A 30-min moderate trek from Gushaini. Log bridges and stone paths make it accessible. Best visited in summer and monsoon.

WaterfallTrekNature
🏡
~8–10 km from Banjar

Jibhi Village

Called "Mini Thailand" for its lush beauty. Famous for Victorian-style wooden tree houses, a charming waterfall, and laid-back Himachali vibe. Gateway to Jalori Pass, Serolsar Lake, and Chehni Kothi. Popular with couples and backpackers.

VillageWaterfallScenic
⛰️
~25 km · via Jibhi

Jalori Pass

At 10,800 ft (3,120 m), this high-altitude pass connects Banjar to Shimla through stunning pine forests. Open April–November. Base for treks to Serolsar Lake and Raghupur Fort. Panoramic 360° Himalayan views. Skiing near Shoja in winter.

PassScenic DriveSnowTrek Base
🏞️
~31 km · 6 km trek from Jalori Pass

Serolsar (Saryolsar) Lake

A sacred high-altitude lake in oak and cedar forest, 6 km trek from Jalori Pass. Home to the revered Budhi Nagin temple. Crystal-clear, snow-fed waters. Perfect for beginner trekkers with stunning Himalayan scenery all around.

LakeTempleEasy Trek
🏯
~28 km · 3 km trek from Jalori Pass

Raghupur Fort

Ruins of an ancient Mandi dynasty fort on a meadow above Jalori Pass. The 360° views of Dhauladhar and Himalayan ranges are breathtaking. Easy 3 km walk from the pass — popular for camping in summer. Mystery surrounds its exact origin and age.

Fort RuinsCampingPanoramic Views
🎣
~10 km · Gushaini village

Tirthan River & Gushaini

The crystal-clear Tirthan River runs through the valley — famous for brown trout fishing (permit required). Gushaini sits at the confluence of Tirthan and Flachan rivers, gateway to GHNP treks. Ideal for riverside walks, picnics, and peaceful mornings.
